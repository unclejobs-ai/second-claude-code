# Research Brief Template

Structured output format for `/second-claude-code:research` findings.

---

## Executive Summary

> [3-5 sentences synthesizing the most important findings. Lead with the single most significant insight. Include the scope of research and confidence level.]

## Key Findings

1. **[Finding title]** -- [2-3 sentence explanation with supporting evidence and source attribution]
2. **[Finding title]** -- [2-3 sentence explanation with supporting evidence and source attribution]
3. **[Finding title]** -- [2-3 sentence explanation with supporting evidence and source attribution]
4. ...

Rules:
- Order by significance, most important first
- Every finding must cite at least one source
- Distinguish facts from interpretations

## Data Points

| Metric | Value | Source | Date | Confidence |
|--------|-------|--------|------|------------|
| [specific metric] | [number] | [source with URL] | [date] | High/Med/Low |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

Rules:
- Only include verifiable numbers from credible sources
- Mark confidence level based on source reliability and corroboration
- Prefer recent data (within 2 years) unless tracking trends

## Analysis

> [3-5 paragraphs connecting the findings into a coherent narrative. Identify patterns, causal relationships, and implications. Distinguish correlation from causation explicitly.]

## Gaps and Limitations

- **[Gap 1]**: [What could not be confirmed and why]
- **[Gap 2]**: [Areas needing deeper investigation]
- **[Limitation 1]**: [Methodological constraints -- e.g., English-only sources, recency bias]

Rules:
- Be honest about what the research did not cover
- Suggest specific follow-up queries for each gap

## Sources

1. [Title](URL) -- accessed [date] -- [one-line relevance note]
2. [Title](URL) -- accessed [date] -- [one-line relevance note]
3. ...

Rules:
- Every URL must come from an actual search result (no fabricated links)
- Include access date for web sources
- Categorize source quality: Tier 1 (primary/official), Tier 2 (reputable secondary), Tier 3 (unverified/opinion)
